Penetration Testing: Complete Methodologies, Tools & Real-World Tactics

By Rehan February 12, 2024 14 min read

Introduction

Penetration testing (ethical hacking) is the art of proactively identifying vulnerabilities before adversaries do. As cyber threats evolve, organizations demand structured, stealthy, and comprehensive security assessments. In this 2024 guide, we dive deep into modern pentesting methodologies ‚Äî from reconnaissance and exploitation to post-exploitation and reporting. Whether you're pursuing OSCP, preparing for red team engagements, or securing your own infrastructure, this guide provides actionable workflows and insider techniques.

The Pentesting Execution Standard (PTES) & Modern Kill Chain

1. Pre-engagement & Intelligence Gathering

Every successful penetration test begins with clear scope and passive reconnaissance. We blend OSINT, certificate transparency logs, and DNS enumeration to build a target map without touching the target.

Essential OSINT toolkit:

  • Shodan / Censys ‚Äì Internet-facing asset discovery
  • theHarvester ‚Äì Email & subdomain collection
  • Amass / Subfinder ‚Äì Attack surface mapping
  • Google dorks & GitHub ‚Äì Leaked credentials/configs
# Passive reconnaissance example (Amass)
amass enum -passive -d target.com -o domains.txt
# Combine with httpx to find live hosts
cat domains.txt | httpx -status-code -title -tech-detect

2. Scanning & Enumeration ‚Äî The Golden Hour

Active scanning requires precision. We avoid noisy scans unless agreed. Modern approach: use version-specific probes and NSE scripts judiciously.

# Stealth SYN scan + service detection + default NSE scripts
nmap -sS -sV -sC -p- --min-rate=500 -T4 target.com

# Targeted vulnerability scan (critical only)
nmap --script vuln --script-args vulns.showCVE -p 80,443,3306 target.com

Pro tip: always check for exposed .git/.env, cloud metadata endpoints, and misconfigured S3 buckets.

Reporting & Remediation

Technical depth is useless without clear business context. We structure reports to help developers fix fast and management understand risk.

  • Executive summary ‚Äì risk appetite, crown jewels exposed
  • Technical walkthrough ‚Äì step-by-step with request/response dumps
  • CVSS 3.1 scoring ‚Äì environmental adjustments
  • Remediation blueprint ‚Äì code snippets, config changes, WAF rules

Red Teaming vs. Pentesting: nuance

While pentests find as many vulnerabilities as possible in a given scope, red team engagements test detection & response. We cover both ‚Äî stealth, evading EDR, and long-term persistence.
